Roller shutters and blinds in Białystok — shade, warmth and peace of mind at home

Good roller shutters are about more than blacking out the bedroom. They add a layer of insulation against the harsh Podlasie winters and act as a real barrier when you're away from home. We'll help you choose the right ones and fit them for you.

External roller shutters

If you're looking for external roller shutters in Białystok, let's start with what they actually do for you. A shutter curtain lowered overnight creates a cushion of air between the window and the aluminium profiles — in winter less heat escapes, and in summer less heat gets in. In our climate, where January nights can drop well below freezing, that's a difference you feel both on your heating bill and in the temperature near the windowsill.

The second reason customers order them is security. A closed shutter with an anti-burglary system and locking bars makes it far harder to lift the curtain from outside — a clear signal that your home isn't left wide open. The third is simply sleep: close to full blackout, with no light leaking down the sides, when the guide rails are properly matched.

Built-on roller shutters

Built-on roller shutters are installed together with the window — the box tucks above the frame and, once the reveals are finished, is usually no longer visible from inside. This is the solution for homes under construction and for window replacements, when you're planning new windows and shutters in a single order. It looks clean and seals tightly, because the whole assembly is designed as one.

Surface-mounted (clip-on) roller shutters

Surface-mounted roller shutters are fitted to a finished façade, above an existing window. The box is visible from outside, but it's the simplest way to add shutters to windows you already have — no chasing into walls, no replacing the frames. They work well when modernising flats and houses where you'd rather not touch the existing joinery.

Adaptive roller shutters

Adaptive roller shutters are the in-between option — the box is concealed in the lintel or within the insulation layer while the building is being renovated and insulated. They come into their own during thermal upgrades: the shutters go in "under the render" and the façade stays clean. If you're planning that kind of project, it's worth thinking about them from the outset, before the crew closes up the walls.

We match each of these types to the specific window and the way it can be fitted — not the other way round. During the survey we check what's physically possible at your property and tell you straight which option makes sense.

Internal blinds and day-and-night blinds

Internal blinds are the lighter layer — fitted directly to the window frame or to the wall, they control light and privacy without touching the façade. They're a good choice wherever you want gentle filtering of the sun in the living room, or to screen a kitchen window from the street.

Day-and-night blinds

Day-and-night blinds are one of the most popular choices today. The fabric has alternating transparent and opaque stripes — by sliding the material, you smoothly adjust how much light comes in. "Day" gives you soft, diffused light with no view in from outside; "night" gives a clear dimming effect. One blind, two modes, with no need to hang separate curtains or nets.

They work beautifully in the bedroom, the children's room or the home office at the screen. We match the stripe width and the fabric shade to your interior — from light beiges through to graphite. If you're also interested in insect protection or controlling light from outside, take a look at our insect screens and venetian blinds — we often combine these in a single order.

Drives and automation

You can operate roller shutters by hand or electrically — a decision we'll make together at the quotation stage. Each approach has its place.

Manual operation

A strap or a crank — inexpensive, reliable, no electricity needed. It makes sense for single ground-floor windows and anywhere you don't need remote control.

Electric drive and remote

A motor inside the shutter barrel, controlled by a wall switch or a remote. It's convenient with large glazed areas, patio windows and anywhere lifting a heavy shutter curtain by hand would be a chore. It's especially handy for older people and for windows that are hard to reach.

Zone control and scenes

Several shutters can be linked into groups and lowered with a single command — for example, every south-facing window at sunset. Some drives work with sensors and with control from your phone, so the shutters can respond to the sun or dusk on their own. If you'd like a setup like this, mention it during the survey — we'll select the right motor and controller.

The specific drive brands and available features we settle on a case-by-case basis, as they depend on the type of shutter and what you're after. We won't promise you a "smart home" where a simple remote will do.

Survey and fitting

Roller shutters have to be measured precisely — a few millimetres decide whether the curtain runs smoothly and whether any light leaks down the sides. That's why we don't sell "by eye". We come to you, measure the windows, assess the wall and the lintel, and only then recommend the type of shutter and the way it should be fitted.
