Roller blinds for windows in Białystok — shade, warmth and peace at home

Good blinds are about more than blacking out the bedroom. They give you gentle control of the light indoors and an extra layer of insulation against the Podlasie frost outside. We'll help you choose the right ones and fit them at your place.

Indoor fabric roller blinds

Fabric roller blinds are the simplest way to take charge of light and privacy in a room — fitted on the window itself or right next to it, with no interference to the façade. The Pro-Plast range has four proven lines of indoor blind: FINEZJA, BESTA, STANDARD and MINI. They differ in how they're mounted and finished, so for every window we'll pick the one that genuinely fits.

FINEZJA — cassette blind with a single guide rail

A blind in an elegant cassette with a single guide rail, screwed or glued to the glazing beads of the sash or to the window frame. The cassette and fabric sit within the glass aperture, and the guide rails are glued to the glazing beads. The mechanism is self-braking — the blind stops wherever you set it, with no cords to tie off. Tidy side end caps are an added bonus. The cassette and aluminium rails come as standard in white and brown; for an extra charge there's silver, plus woodgrain finishes: light oak, oak, golden oak, mahogany and cherry.

BESTA — twin-rail cassette, ideal for older buildings

BESTA is a blind in a cassette with two guide rails, glued to the sash or window frame. Unlike FINEZJA, the cassette does not sit within the glass aperture, and once the blind is rolled up it becomes completely invisible. It has a modern, distinctive shape and is glued on in full or hung on special brackets, so it doesn't "damage" the window. It's the ideal solution for older buildings and anywhere you don't want to drill into the frame. The cassette and aluminium rails come as standard in white, silver and brown; from the RAL palette for an extra charge.

STANDARD and MINI — free-hanging blinds, 11 fitting colours

STANDARD and MINI are free-hanging blinds that can be mounted anywhere — to the reveal, the wall or the ceiling. Both have a self-braking mechanism and a neat, rounded shape. MINI is the smaller version of STANDARD — it works well on small windows and anywhere you want a compact cassette. The fittings come in as many as 11 colours: white, grey, brown, pink, yellow, beige, salmon, light green and dark green, navy and light blue — it's easy to match the shade to your interior.

Not sure which line makes sense for you? During the survey we'll show you fabric and colour samples and give you concrete advice. If you're also interested in controlling the light with slats, take a look at venetian blinds, and insect screens will keep the bugs out — we often combine these in a single order.

Outdoor roller shutters — warmth, privacy and security

Outdoor roller shutters are far more than blackout. Lowered for the night, the shutter curtain creates an air cushion between the window and the shutter — in winter less heat escapes, in summer less of the heat gets in. In our climate, where January nights drop well below freezing, that's a difference you feel both on the heating bill and in the temperature by the sill.

The second reason is privacy and security. A closed shutter screens the interior from the eyes of passers-by, and the anti-burglary versions with locking bars make it harder to force the curtain up from outside — a visible sign that the house isn't standing wide open. We make the curtain from foam-filled aluminium profiles, which are light, rigid and add sound insulation on top.

Top-mounted roller shutters

Top-mounted shutters are fitted together with the window — the box tucks above the frame and, once the reveals are finished, is usually no longer visible from inside. This is the solution for homes under construction and for window replacements, when you're planning new windows and shutters in a single order. It comes out neat and seals tightly, because we design the whole assembly as one.

Adaptive roller shutters

Adaptive shutters are a version fitted to a finished façade, above an existing window — a way to add shutters to windows you already have, with no chasing into walls and no replacing the joinery. They work well when modernising flats and houses where you'd rather not touch the existing frames.

Built-in (under-render) roller shutters

Built-in shutters are concealed within the insulation layer while the building is being renovated and insulated. They come into their own during thermal upgrades: the box goes "under the render" and the façade stays clean, with no protruding parts. When planning a project like this it's worth thinking about them from the outset, before the crew closes up the walls.

Operation — manual or automated?

You can operate outdoor shutters by hand or electrically — a decision we'll make together at the quotation stage. Each approach has its place.

Manual operation

A strap or a crank — inexpensive, reliable, no electricity needed. It makes sense for single ground-floor windows and anywhere you don't need remote control. The indoor blinds FINEZJA, STANDARD and MINI run on a self-braking mechanism, so they stop in your chosen position with no extra effort.

Automation — motor and remote

A motor inside the shutter barrel, controlled by a remote or a wall switch. It's convenient with large glazed areas, patio windows and anywhere lifting a heavy shutter curtain by hand would be a chore. It's especially handy for older people and for windows that are hard to reach. Several shutters can be linked into groups and lowered with a single command — for example, every south-facing window at sunset.

The specific drive brands and available features we settle on a case-by-case basis, as they depend on the type of shutter and what you're after. We won't promise you a "smart home" where a simple remote will do.

Survey and fitting in Białystok and the surrounding area

Blinds have to be measured precisely — a few millimetres decide whether the curtain runs smoothly and whether any light leaks down the sides. That's why we don't sell "by eye". We come to you, measure the windows, assess the wall and the lintel, and only then recommend the type of blind and the way it should be fitted.

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between FINEZJA and BESTA blinds?
FINEZJA has a cassette with a single guide rail that's screwed or glued to the glazing beads — the cassette and fabric sit within the glass aperture. BESTA has a cassette with two guide rails, doesn't sit within the glass aperture and is completely invisible once rolled up. BESTA is glued on in full or hung, so it doesn't damage the window and is perfectly suited to older buildings.
What colours of indoor blind are available?
FINEZJA: white and brown as standard, with silver plus the woodgrain finishes light oak, oak, golden oak, mahogany and cherry for an extra charge. BESTA: white, silver and brown as standard, and from the RAL palette for an extra charge. STANDARD and MINI have fittings in 11 colours: white, grey, brown, pink, yellow, beige, salmon, light green and dark green, navy and light blue.
Can outdoor roller shutters be controlled by remote?
Yes. We offer outdoor shutters with manual operation (strap or crank) or with automation — a motor in the barrel controlled by a remote or a wall switch. Automation is especially convenient with large glazed areas, patio windows and for older people. Several shutters can also be linked into groups and lowered with a single command.
Can blinds be added to windows I already have?
Yes. For existing windows we fit adaptive outdoor shutters — the box is mounted above the window on the finished façade, with no chasing into walls and no replacing the joinery. Indoors, BESTA fabric blinds work well, as they glue to the window with no drilling. During the survey we check the wall and the lintel and tell you straight which type of blind can be fitted at your property.
How much do roller blinds cost in Białystok?
The price depends on the type of blind (indoor fabric or outdoor), the window dimensions, the type of curtain, the colours and how it's operated — manual or automated. For that reason we don't publish a fixed price list. After a free on-site survey you'll receive a clear, binding quotation with no hidden items. Instalment payments are available.
What's the warranty, and do you fit blinds in winter?
We give a 5-year warranty on our products, and after fitting there's ongoing after-sales support. We fit blinds through the winter season too — in the Podlasie climate that's a normal part of our work. We arrange the date individually, around the conditions and your availability.
