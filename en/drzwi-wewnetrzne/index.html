Interior doors in Białystok — matched to your home

Inside the house, different things matter than outside: a quiet room, easy cleaning and a leaf that still closes squarely years later. We'll help you match the doors to your interior, measure up at your place and fit them with a frame to suit the wall.

Types of interior door — framed or panel?

Choosing interior doors starts with the leaf construction, because that's what determines the weight, the sound insulation and the price. In practice you're choosing between two basic types:

  • Framed doors — a light frame of timber or wood-based board faced with finishing panels, with an infill (for example a honeycomb cardboard core or a board). Lighter and usually better value — well suited to rooms and utility spaces.
  • Panel (solid) doors — more substantial, with a board infill, better at dampening sound and feeling more solid. A good choice for a bedroom or study where you want quiet.

We'll advise you which type makes sense in each room — a bathroom door is chosen differently from a statement living-room door. On the entrance side, it's worth matching them to the exterior doors so the home reads as a coherent whole.

Finishes: foil, laminate or lacquer?

The leaf finish governs the look, the scratch resistance and how the door stands up to everyday use. You'll most often come across three kinds:

  • Foil — a natural or synthetic coating that imitates wood; it gives a warm, decorative effect and a rich choice of patterns.
  • Laminate — a hard, abrasion-resistant surface that's easy to clean; good for homes with children and heavily used spaces.
  • Lacquer — a smooth, uniform finish, often in white or colours; elegant, though it calls for slightly gentler handling.

For the bathroom and kitchen we choose finishes that withstand moisture and steam, with a ventilation undercut or air-flow sleeves. We'll tell you plainly which collections cope well with heavy use and which are best treated more gently.

Rebated or flush — which to choose?

Interior doors come in two rebate variants — that is, the way the leaf meets the frame:

Rebated doors

The leaf overlaps the frame with a rebate strip that hides the gap. It's a classic, proven and usually more affordable solution — it dampens sound well and forgives small fitting imperfections.

Flush doors

The leaf sits flush with the frame in a single plane, and the hinges are often concealed. The effect is modern and minimalist, with the door looking as if it's set into the wall. They call for precise fitting — and that's where the experience of the crew, which you get included with us, makes the difference.

We'll suggest which variant suits your interior and budget, and how it works with the rest of the finish.

Frames, glazing and colours

A door is more than the leaf — a frame matched to the wall thickness can matter just as much. We use adjustable frames that adapt to varying wall thicknesses (typical in renovated flats with uneven walls) and fixed frames where they work best.

If you want light in the corridor or deeper into the flat, we'll choose leaves with glazing — from narrow strips to large panes of safety glass. Colours run from white and classic wood shades to fashionable greys and graphite, so we can easily match the doors to the flooring, furniture and skirting. We'll match the handles and hardware to the style of the rest of your fittings.

Brand-name makers, measurement and fitting

We work with proven Polish manufacturers of interior doors, which gives us a wide choice of types, finishes and colours for every budget. Our range includes doors by DRE, Erkado, Pol-Skone, Porta and Perfectdoor.

  1. Advice and matching to your interior — we help you choose the type, finish and colour to suit your floors, furniture and the style of the flat.

Frequently asked questions

How much do interior doors cost fitted in Białystok?
The price depends on the brand and model, the leaf type (framed or panel), the finish, the type of frame and the number of doors, so we won't quote a fixed price blind. The best approach is to book a free measurement — we'll prepare a free, firm quote with fitting. You can spread the cost over instalments.
What's the difference between rebated and flush doors?
With rebated doors the leaf overlaps the frame with a rebate strip that hides the gap — it's the classic variant, good at dampening sound and more affordable. With flush doors the leaf sits flush with the frame in a single plane, often with concealed hinges, giving a modern effect but calling for precise fitting.
Which doors work best for a bathroom?
For the bathroom and kitchen we choose finishes that withstand moisture and steam — most often laminate — and leaves with a ventilation undercut or air-flow sleeves that allow airflow. We'll recommend a specific collection during the measurement.
What is an adjustable frame and when is it used?
An adjustable frame adapts to varying wall thicknesses, which makes it convenient in renovated flats with uneven walls and wherever the wall thickness has changed after finishing. Where conditions allow, we also use fixed frames. We'll choose the right type during the measurement.
Will you help me match the doors to the floor and furniture?
Yes — that's part of our advice. We have a wide palette of colours and finishes, from white and wood shades to greys and graphite, so we'll match the doors to the flooring, skirting and furniture. We'll show you samples and help you choose the leaf type and handles to suit the style of your interior.
Do you fit interior doors onto existing frames?
It depends on the condition and type of the old frame and on the chosen leaf — sometimes replacing just the leaf is enough, but more often we recommend replacing the whole set for an even fit and proper adjustment. We'll assess this during the free measurement and propose the most sensible solution.
