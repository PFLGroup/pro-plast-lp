Pleated Insect Screens in Białystok — a Concertina for Doors and Wide Openings

A pleated insect screen folds the mesh like a concertina and slides open smoothly, in a single move. It is a solution for balcony and terrace doors and wide openings we pass through many times a day. We measure and fit to size in Białystok and the surrounding area.

How Does a Pleated Insect Screen Work?

A pleated insect screen is a mesh that folds like a concertina (a pleat), guided in a bottom and a top track. You slide it to the side like a pleated blind — gently, in a single move — and when it is not needed it folds down to a few centimetres at the side guide. Hence the second name: concertina insect screens.

The top and bottom tracks hold the mesh evenly and guide it smoothly across the full width, even in very wide openings. Among our insect screens this is the premium option — chosen by those who value the comfort of an everyday crossing and a modern look.

For Balcony and Terrace Doors and Wide Openings

A pleat works best where a window frame is no longer enough — at balcony and terrace doors and wide openings we walk through rather than just tilt open. The slim profile suits large terrace doors and corner glazing, and the smooth sliding makes the crossing comfortable even dozens of times a day.

Frequently asked questions

What is a pleated insect screen?
A pleated insect screen is a mesh that folds like a concertina (a pleat), guided in a bottom and a top track. You slide it to the side in a single move, and when it is not needed it folds down to a few centimetres at the side guide. Hence the second name: concertina insect screens.
Which doors and openings does a pleated insect screen fit?
A pleat works best at balcony and terrace doors and wide openings we often pass through. The bottom and top tracks guide the mesh evenly across the full width, so a pleat also handles very wide openings onto the terrace and garden.
Does a pleated insect screen have a high threshold?
No. Pleated insect screens have a low threshold that makes crossing easier — also with a pushchair. The mesh slides open smoothly in a single move, so the crossing is comfortable even when you use the door dozens of times a day.

It depends on the width of the opening and how often you pass through it. A pleat gives a slim look and smooth sliding, while a sliding insect screen suits very large glazing. During the free survey we will advise which version is more convenient for your entrance.
