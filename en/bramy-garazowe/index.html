Garage Doors in Białystok — sectional BIG-TOR and roller

Garage doors matched to your driveway and your garage — warm, quiet and easy to live with day to day. We'll fit a proven BIG-TOR ISO 40G sectional door, or a roller door when there's no room under the ceiling. We measure up, advise and fit.

BIG-TOR ISO 40G sectional door

A sectional door is now the most common choice for single and double garages, and the BIG-TOR ISO 40G pairs a modern look with comfort and practicality. The curtain is made up of horizontal panels joined by hinges that lift up and tuck away under the ceiling as the door opens. Nothing sticks out beyond the line of the garage — you can happily park a car right in front of the door, and the driveway doesn't need to be long. The door is sound-absorbing and highly thermally insulating.

At the heart of the door is insulation of polyurethane (PUR) foam 40 mm thick. Our winters can be hard going, and a garage usually sits right against the house — a thick panel limits heat loss and quietens the inside. If the garage is heated or connected to the living space, that genuinely matters.

Construction, panels and safety features

The BIG-TOR ISO 40G is a door built with durability and everyday safety in mind. Every part of the construction has been thought through here — from the steel panels to the spring safety devices.

Panels and coating

The curtain is formed from panels 500 mm and/or 610 mm high, insulated with PUR foam 40 mm thick. The panel skins are steel, galvanised and polyester-coated — a coating resistant to corrosion and the weather that holds its colour and looks for years.

Springs and durability

The door runs on torsion springs rated for a minimum of 15 000 cycles. In practice that's many years of daily opening and closing without losing smoothness of operation.

Safety features

  • Finger-trap protection — the design of the panel sections limits the risk of being caught as the door folds.
  • Spring-break protection — in the event of a spring failure it stops the door curtain from dropping suddenly.

The door can be opened manually — using a handle or a pull cord — or with an electric drive. Whichever you choose, the BIG-TOR works smoothly and quietly.

Colours, textures and finishes

A rich palette of textures, colours and finishes lets you match the door to the façade and architecture of a detached house, so that together they form a coherent, attractive whole. We match the panels to the character of the façade and the front door — in Białystok customers most often go for anthracite, brown or white, and the surface can also be given a woodgrain texture if the rest of the joinery leans that way.

The door colours are easy to coordinate with your windows and external doors — then the front of the building reads as one. During the survey we'll show you texture and finish samples, so the decision is based on what you'll actually see rather than a catalogue photo.

Roller doors — when there's no room under the ceiling

A roller door works differently from a sectional one — instead of lifting up to the ceiling, it rolls up into a box above the opening, much like external roller blinds. It's the answer for garages where there's no room under the ceiling, or where you want to use the ceiling for storage, lighting or services.

The roller curtain is made up of narrow aluminium slats, usually foam-filled. A roller door takes up minimal space inside the garage and gives you the freedom to park right beneath it. It also works where the lintel is low and a classic door would clash with a car standing in front of the garage.

When choosing between a BIG-TOR sectional door and a roller door, we go by what you've got on site: the height of the lintel, the room under the ceiling, how the garage is used and your budget. At the survey we'll show you which solution comes out better for you, rather than pushing the pricier option for the sake of it.

Drives and remotes

A garage door can be opened by hand, but in practice almost every customer goes for an electric drive. You pull up to the garage, press the remote and drive straight in without getting out of the car — in winter and in the rain, that makes a difference. We match the drive to the size and weight of the door, so it runs smoothly and isn't worked too hard day to day.

As standard the drive comes with remotes, and the opening can be extended with control from your phone, an internal button by the door into the house, or a keypad outside. If you have a particular setup in mind — a shared remote for the garage door and the gate, say — mention it when we quote and we'll put the kit together for it. The drive is also about break-in protection: with the door closed it locks it mechanically, so it can't easily be lifted from outside. In a power cut you can open the door by hand thanks to the emergency release, which can matter during winter outages.

Survey and fitting in Białystok and the surrounding area

A door that works well starts with a proper survey. We come to you, check the width and height of the opening, the depth of the garage, the height of the lintel and the room under the ceiling, as well as the state of the reveals and how the services are run. Only on that basis do we settle the type of door, the track layout and the drive. The survey and quote are free.

The fitting is done by our own crew — the same tradesmen who fit windows and doors, so they know the realities of Białystok houses, detached garages and semis. We set the door true to the vertical and horizontal, seal it, adjust the springs and the drive, and at the end show you how to use the controls and how to release the door by hand. We also take care of aftercare — if something needs adjusting after a season, we come back.

We fit all year round, winter included. A garage door itself is less sensitive to this than wet fitting, so frost usually doesn't hold up a job — what matters is the state of the opening and a place prepared for the drive. We also offer delivery, sale by instalments and a 5-year warranty. For the details and a survey date get in touch or call: 602 488 413 or 85 746 14 46.

Frequently asked questions

What sets the BIG-TOR ISO 40G sectional door apart?
The BIG-TOR ISO 40G is a sectional door with panels 500/610 mm high, insulated with PUR foam 40 mm thick. The panel skins are steel, galvanised and polyester-coated, and the door runs on torsion springs rated for a minimum of 15 000 cycles. It's sound-absorbing and highly thermally insulating, and a rich palette of textures, colours and finishes lets you match it to the façade.
How warm is the BIG-TOR ISO 40G door?
The door panels are insulated with polyurethane (PUR) foam 40 mm thick, which gives high thermal insulation and sound absorption. That matters especially in the Podlasie climate, when the garage sits against a heated house or is connected to the living space — a thick panel genuinely limits heat loss.
What safety features does the garage door have?
The BIG-TOR ISO 40G has finger-trap protection on the panel sections and spring-break protection that stops the curtain from dropping suddenly if a spring fails. A closed door locks mechanically, making it hard to lift from outside. In a power cut you can open it by hand thanks to the emergency release.
Sectional or roller door — which should I choose for my garage?
The BIG-TOR sectional door tucks away under the ceiling and works well where there's room above for the tracks. A roller door rolls up into a box over the opening and is better when the lintel is low, the ceiling is sloped, or you want to use the space under the ceiling. The choice comes down to the height of the lintel, the room under the ceiling and how the garage is used — we check this at the survey and give you concrete advice.
How much does a garage door cost in Białystok?
The price depends on the type of door (BIG-TOR sectional or roller), its dimensions, the colour and finish, and the drive and safety extras. A standard single garage is quoted differently from a double door with an electric drive. That's why we start with a free on-site survey and quote — we give you a firm figure once we've seen the opening. Purchase by instalments is also possible.
Do you fit garage doors in winter?
Yes, we fit all year round, including through bitter Podlasie winters. Fitting a door is less sensitive to low temperatures than wet work, so frost usually doesn't hold up a job. What counts above all is the state of the opening and a place prepared for the drive. After fitting we show you how to use it and adjust the door, and if something needs touching up after a season, we come back as part of aftercare.
