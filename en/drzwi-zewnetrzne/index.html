Exterior doors in Białystok — warm, secure, built to last

Your front door is the first thing a guest sees and the last barrier between the cold outside and the warmth indoors. We'll help you choose it, measure up at your place and fit it so it holds the heat and properly protects your home.

Exterior doors — which ones are genuinely warm?

With winters as harsh as they get in Podlasie, you can spot a good exterior door by one figure: the heat transfer coefficient Ud — the lower it is, the less heat escapes through the entrance. A warm door means more than a smaller heating bill: no cold draught in the hallway and less risk of condensation on the threshold.

A door's warmth comes down to several things at once:

  • Insulated leaf infill — an insulating core that limits heat loss through the face of the door.
  • A warm threshold (thermal threshold) — with a thermal break that separates the cold side from the warm one and prevents water condensing at the bottom of the door.
  • Tight perimeter seals — a well-fitting leaf means no draught even in windy weather.

In Białystok and the surrounding area, a warm door pays for itself fastest where you're replacing an old, draughty entrance — you'll feel the difference in the very first winter. During the free measurement we'll match the model to whether the door opens straight outdoors or is sheltered by a covered porch.

Security and anti-burglary classes

We build security in layers, not around a single catchy slogan. Protection for an entrance door comes from a reinforced leaf and frame, multi-point locks that bolt the door in several places at once, anti-prise pins on the hinge side, and a solid cylinder with anti-snap and anti-drill protection.

Exterior joinery uses anti-burglary classes (the most common being RC2 and RC3), which describe how long a door resists a break-in attempt with typical tools. We won't pretend any door is impossible to force. Here's the honest version instead: a well-chosen anti-burglary door in Białystok significantly increases the time a break-in takes, and that's usually enough to put an intruder off. We'll match the class and level of protection to whether you live in a block of flats or a house on the edge of town.

Materials, glazing and side lights

We match exterior doors to the building's conditions and style. Most often you'll come across three material options:

Steel, aluminium or PVC?

  • Steel — the classic basis for entrance doors: durable, rigid and with a good price-to-longevity ratio.
  • Aluminium — light and weather-resistant, allowing modern, slim designs and large formats; a frequent choice for homes with plenty of glass.
  • PVC — practical, moisture-resistant and easy to look after, a sensible option for a side or utility entrance, for instance.

Light in the hallway

If you want a bright hallway, we'll choose a door with glazing in the leaf or with side lights — side panels or a glazed top panel that lets light in without compromising security. The glass in exterior doors is toughened and insulated, so it doesn't undermine the door's overall insulation.

Colours, finishes and brand-name makers

We work with proven Polish manufacturers of exterior doors, which gives us a wide choice of designs, classes and finishes for every budget. Our range includes doors by Wikęd, Barański Drzwi, Erkado, Delta, Setto and Doorsy.

Designs run from smooth, modern leaves, through models with subtle grooving, to versions with stainless-steel detailing. Colours and finishes include classic anthracite grey, warm wood-effect foils and palettes in RAL shades. If you're replacing your PVC windows at the same time, it's worth matching the entrance to the rest of the joinery — we'll show you the samples side by side so the front of the building comes together as a whole. On the inside, we'll match it with suitable interior doors.

Advice, measurement and fitting

Even the best door, poorly seated, stops being the best door. A leaf set out of true, missing insulation where it meets the wall, or a badly adjusted threshold and you get draughts despite great figures on the label. That's why with us it's a single process from start to finish — from advice, through measurement, to fitting by our own crew.

Frequently asked questions

How much do exterior doors cost fitted in Białystok?
The price depends on the brand and model, the thermal class (Ud) and anti-burglary class, the material and the state of the opening, so we won't quote a fixed price blind. The best approach is to book a free measurement — on that basis we'll prepare a free, firm quote with delivery and fitting. You can spread the cost over instalments.
How warm are the exterior doors you offer?
A door's warmth comes down to its Ud value (the lower the better), an insulated leaf infill, a warm thermal threshold and tight seals. We'll give you the exact Ud figure for the chosen model with the quote — in Podlasie winters it's a parameter that genuinely affects your heating bills.
What's the difference between anti-burglary classes RC2 and RC3?
The RC classes (most often RC2 and RC3) describe how long a door resists a break-in attempt with typical tools — RC3 offers a higher level of resistance than RC2. No door is impossible to force, but a well-chosen class significantly lengthens the time a break-in takes and deters an intruder. We'll help you pick the right class for a flat or a detached house.
Which entrance door material should I choose — steel, aluminium or PVC?
Steel is a durable, economical basis for entrance doors; aluminium is light, weather-resistant and allows modern, slim designs and large glazing; PVC works well where moisture resistance and easy upkeep matter most, such as a side entrance. We'll advise you during the measurement.
Can the door be matched to the colour of the windows?
Yes. We have a wide palette of colours and finishes — from anthracite to wood-effect foils and RAL shades — and we'll show you the samples side by side so the entrance matches the windows and façade. If you're also planning to replace the windows, we'll combine both jobs into a single visit.
What guarantee do your exterior doors come with?
We provide a 5-year guarantee on the doors, and our own crew carries out the fitting with care for sealing and correct seating. As a local Białystok firm we also offer after-sales care — if the hardware or threshold ever needs adjusting, we're on hand.
