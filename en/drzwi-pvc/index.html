PVC Doors in Białystok — terrace, balcony and side entrance

PVC doors combine the tight seal of a window with everyday convenience. You see it best on a large terrace glazing: a leaf that slides easily, a low threshold with nothing to trip over, and warmth that stays inside the house.

HS terrace and balcony doors — huge glazing, effortless slide

HS terrace doors (tilt-and-slide, lift-and-slide) are the most comfortable way out to a terrace or garden you can build from PVC. The huge leaf with its large pane of glass lets a great deal of light into the living room, and thanks to the HS-Portal hardware it slides easily, without forcing — even with a heavy, glazed leaf. Once lifted, the leaf rises above the threshold and runs on aluminium tracks, so it stays smooth to operate for years.

We base the construction on a Salamander profile with HS-Portal hardware. It is a solution for genuinely large openings: up to 2.5 m high and 5 m wide, with a leaf weighing up to 400 kg. Security and a tight seal are held by 4 locking points as standard.

As standard we fit an insulated glass unit 24 mm wide with a coefficient of Ug = 1,1 W/m²K, and we select the glazing unit within the 24–44 mm range — to give the thermal and acoustic insulation you need. Looking for even larger glazing or an aluminium version? See HS ALU terrace doors.

Low threshold — convenience and step-free access

A low, energy-efficient threshold is one of the biggest advantages of HS terrace doors. There is nothing to catch your foot on, it is easy to roll a pushchair, a child's bike or garden furniture across, and the line between living room and terrace almost disappears. The threshold is suitable for people with disabilities and for older people — real accessibility, not a brochure slogan.

A low threshold in an external door has to be both tight and warm, so there is no draught along the floor in winter. That is why we settle how the threshold is seated and how its junction with the floor is finished during the measurement — in a home near Białystok, where winter lasts for months, this detail decides the comfort.

Side-hung and tilt-and-turn balcony doors

Not every way out needs to be a large sliding glazing. For a flat balcony or a smaller terrace, classic side-hung balcony doors or tilt-and-turn doors are often a better fit — opened inwards like a window leaf, with the option of tilting for ventilation.

  • Side-hung — the leaf opens fully, simple to operate, ideal where there is room to open inwards.
  • Tilt-and-turn — one leaf, two modes: a full opening and a micro-tilt for airing without chilling the room.

We make them from the same brand-name PVC profiles as our PVC windows, so the balcony and the windows in the room match in colour and performance as one whole.

Side entrance doors in PVC

For a utility entrance, garage, boiler room, laundry or a garden-side gate, PVC side entrance doors work well. They are practical wherever resistance to moisture and minimal upkeep matter: no painting or varnishing, easy to wipe down, good with rain and temperature swings.

We make them solid or with a glazed light, in colours from a foil palette matched to the rest of the joinery. They are a sensible choice where timber would quickly suffer and durability and convenience come first. If you are looking for a representative door for the main entrance, take a look at our brand-name exterior doors.

Colours, finish and upkeep

PVC doors do not have to be white. We finish the leaf and frame with decorative films or lacquer — from white, through anthracite, to warm wood-effect foils. We match the colour so the terrace doors tie in with the windows and the façade.

Upkeep is simple: just wipe PVC down with a damp cloth, and grease the seals and hardware once a season — on sliding HS doors this keeps the slide light for years. At handover we will show you how to look after them yourself.

Measurement, fitting and guarantee

The best terrace doors, poorly seated, stop being the best — with a large, heavy HS leaf, correct fitting matters even more than in a window. That is why we handle the whole job, from measurement to adjustment, with our own crew.

Frequently asked questions

How large can PVC HS terrace doors be?
We make HS terrace doors on a Salamander profile up to 2.5 m high and 5 m wide, with a leaf weighing up to 400 kg. Despite their size the leaf slides easily thanks to the HS-Portal hardware and its run on aluminium tracks. We'll work out the exact dimensions at the free measurement.
Is the threshold on terrace doors really low?
Yes. HS terrace doors have a low, energy-efficient threshold suitable for people with disabilities and older people — no barrier to trip over, easy to roll a pushchair across. During fitting we make sure the threshold is both warm and tight, which matters in the winters of the Podlasie region.
How warm are PVC terrace doors?
As standard we fit a 24 mm insulated glass unit with a coefficient of Ug = 1,1 W/m²K, and we select the glazing unit within the 24–44 mm range for thermal and acoustic insulation. The Salamander HS profile has a frame depth of 172 mm and a leaf depth of 76 mm, which gives a good seal on large glazing.
How do HS doors differ from side-hung balcony doors?
HS doors are a large tilt-and-slide (lift-and-slide) leaf with a low threshold — for big terraces. Side-hung or tilt-and-turn balcony doors open inwards like a window leaf and suit balconies and smaller openings. We'll advise which works better for you.
Do you also make PVC side entrance doors?
Yes. PVC side entrance doors work well for a garage, boiler room, laundry or a garden-side entrance — resistant to moisture, no painting needed and easy to wipe down. For a representative door at the main entrance, see our range of brand-name exterior doors.
How much do PVC terrace doors cost fitted in Białystok?
The price depends on the dimensions, the type (HS sliding, side-hung balcony, side entrance), the glazing unit and the colour, so we won't quote a fixed price blind. It's best to book a free measurement — on that basis we'll prepare a free quote in writing. The purchase can be spread over instalments.
