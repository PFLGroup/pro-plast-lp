External Roller Shutters in Białystok — Warmth, Privacy and Security

External roller shutters are an aluminium curtain in front of the window that protects against heat in summer and cold in winter, gives privacy and improves anti-burglary protection. We fit them as built-on, surface-mounted or plaster-in, with manual control or automation.

External Roller Shutters — Why Fit Them?

External roller shutters are far more than just shading. A lowered curtain creates an extra layer in front of the window that works all year round and answers four specific needs at once.

  • Protection from heat in summer and cold in winter — the curtain limits the interior heating up in hot spells and heat escaping in frosts.
  • Privacy — a closed shutter screens the interior from view from outside.
  • Improved anti-burglary protection — the curtain makes it harder to reach the window from the facade.
  • Noise reduction — the extra layer dampens noise from the street.

We make the curtain from foam-filled aluminium profiles — they are light, rigid and additionally reduce noise. In the Podlasie climate, where the winter can be harsh and the summer scorching, this single investment works towards comfort all year round.

Types of External Roller Shutters — Built-On, Surface-Mounted, Plaster-In

We fit external roller shutters in three versions, depending on whether you are building, renovating or adding a shutter to an existing window. We decide on the type together during the survey.

Built-on shutters

Built-on shutters are fitted together with the window — the box is concealed above the frame and is usually invisible from inside once the reveals are finished. It is the solution for homes under construction and for joinery replacement, when you are planning new windows and shutters in a single order — the result is neat and airtight, because we design the whole arrangement as a whole.

Surface-mounted shutters

Surface-mounted shutters are fitted on a finished facade, above an existing window — it is a way to add shutters to windows you already have, without breaking through walls or replacing joinery. They work well when modernising homes and flats.

Plaster-in shutters

Plaster-in shutters are hidden in the insulation layer during a renovation and the insulating of the building. The box goes "under the plaster", and the facade stays clean, with no protruding parts. When carrying out thermal modernisation, it is worth thinking about them straight away, before the crew closes up the walls.

Control — Manual or Automation?

You operate external roller shutters manually or electrically — a decision we will make together at the quote stage, because each method has its place.

Manual control

A strap or a crank — cheap, reliable, with no electricity. It makes sense for single ground-floor windows and where you do not need remote control.

Automation — motor and remote

A motor in the shutter tube and control via a remote or a wall switch. Convenient for large glazing, terrace windows and where raising a heavy curtain by hand would be a chore. It works especially well for the elderly and at hard-to-reach windows.

The specific drive brands and the functions available are agreed individually, because they depend on the type of shutter and your expectations. We do not promise a "smart home" where a simple remote is all you need.

Frequently asked questions

What benefits do external roller shutters give?
External roller shutters protect against heat in summer and cold in winter, give privacy, improve anti-burglary protection and reduce noise inside. We make the curtain from foam-filled aluminium profiles, which are light, rigid and additionally dampen noise. This solution works towards comfort all year round.
How do built-on, surface-mounted and plaster-in shutters differ?
Built-on shutters are fitted together with the window — the box is concealed above the frame. Surface-mounted shutters are fitted on a finished facade, above an existing window, without breaking through walls or replacing joinery. Plaster-in shutters are hidden in the insulation layer during a renovation and insulating, so the facade stays clean. We choose the type during the survey.
Can external roller shutters be controlled by remote?
Yes. We offer external roller shutters with manual control (a strap or a crank) or with automation — a motor in the tube controlled by a remote or a wall switch. Automation is especially convenient with large glazing, terrace windows and for the elderly. We agree the specific drives and functions individually.
Can external roller shutters be added to existing windows?
Yes. For existing windows we fit surface-mounted shutters — the box is fixed on a finished facade, above the window, without breaking through walls or replacing joinery. During the survey we check the wall and the lintel and tell you plainly which type of external shutter can be fitted at your place.
