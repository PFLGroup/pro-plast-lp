Framed Insect Screens in Białystok — the Classic for Windows, Made to Measure

A framed insect screen is the simplest and most popular way to keep mosquitoes and midges out of your window. We make the rigid aluminium frame to measure, and the clip-on fitting needs no drilling into the joinery. We measure and fit in Białystok and the surrounding area.

What Is a Framed Insect Screen?

A framed insect screen is a taut mesh set in a rigid aluminium frame, made to measure for a specific window. It is a solution for tilt-and-turn windows — bedrooms, kitchens, living rooms — anywhere you want to let in fresh air but not insects. The frame is light yet rigid, so the mesh stays evenly taut all season.

The standard is a fibreglass mesh — flexible, unbreakable and UV-resistant, so it does not yellow or grow brittle after years in the sun. We match the frame colour to the joinery, so the screen does not catch the eye. Of all our insect screens, the framed type is the cheapest and the most frequently chosen.

Frame Colours and Fitting Without Drilling

We make the aluminium frame in three colours: white, brown and a golden oak shade, so we can match it to white, brown or wood-effect joinery. As a result the screen looks like part of the window rather than an add-on forced on top.

Clip-On Fitting — No Drilling in the Frame

We fit the framed screen on clips mounted on the outer frame — we do not drill into the window frame itself, so the joinery warranty stays intact. The clips hold the frame firmly and at the same time let you take it off in a few seconds, with no tools.

  • A rigid aluminium frame made to measure from the survey.
  • 3 frame colours: white, brown, golden oak.
  • A fibreglass mesh — flexible and UV-resistant.
  • Clip-on fitting, no drilling into the window frame.

Easy Removal for Winter

Framed insect screens are removable, and that is a real advantage. In spring you put them on, in autumn you take them off in a few seconds — with no tools — and store them in the cellar, attic or garage. As a result the mesh does not work under snow and ice in winter, does not collect dirt out of season and keeps a clean look for years.

This is the solution for those who do not mind the seasonal taking-off and who value the best price and simplicity. If you prefer a version that does not need removing at all, take a look at roller and pleated insect screens — there the mesh hides in a cassette or folds at the guide and stays in place all year.

Frequently asked questions

What colours is the framed insect screen frame available in?
The aluminium frame of the framed insect screen comes in three colours: white, brown and a golden oak shade. This lets us match it to the colour of the joinery, so the screen does not catch the eye. We make every frame to measure from the survey.
Does fitting a framed insect screen require drilling into the window?
No. We fit framed insect screens on clips mounted on the outer frame, with no drilling into the window frame itself. As a result your joinery warranty stays intact, and the frame can be taken off at any time without tools.
Do framed insect screens have to be removed for winter?
Framed insect screens are removable — in spring you put them on, in autumn you take them off in a few seconds without tools and store them in the cellar or garage. As a result the mesh does not work under snow in winter and keeps a clean look for many seasons.
What mesh are framed insect screens made of?
The standard is a fibreglass mesh — flexible, unbreakable and UV-resistant, so it does not yellow or grow brittle in the sun. It effectively keeps out mosquitoes and midges while letting fresh air through.
Which windows do framed insect screens fit?
Framed insect screens fit any type of tilt-and-turn window. We make them to measure for a specific opening, so we can match them to windows in the bedroom, kitchen or living room. For terrace and balcony doors we rather recommend door or pleated insect screens.
