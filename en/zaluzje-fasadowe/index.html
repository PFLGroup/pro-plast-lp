Façade Blinds (external) in Białystok

Blinds fitted on the elevation that stop the sun before it heats the glass — they protect the interior from overheating in summer. Slat-angle control, manual or automatic operation. The on-site survey and quote are free.

Fitted on the elevation — shading before the sun heats the glass

Façade blinds are fitted outside, on the building's elevation, in front of the glass — and that's the fundamental difference from internal shading. They stop the sun's rays before they heat the glass, so the warmth doesn't get inside. In practice that means less stuffy afternoons, lower room temperatures and less work for the air conditioning.

Internal shading reacts once the glass has already warmed up; a façade blind acts earlier, so it protects the interior from overheating in summer far more effectively. It's a solution for homes and offices with large glazing, especially on the south and west sides.

Adjustment, control and privacy

Façade blinds give smooth control over light and the view. You set the tilting slats to let in diffused light, reduce it to a shade or cover the interior completely — without retracting the blind.

  • Protection against overheating — they reflect the sun's rays before they heat the glass and the interior.
  • Slat-angle control — a smooth transition from full daylight to shade.
  • Privacy — you can see out, but no one can see in from the street.
  • Façade aesthetics — a modern look that sits well with large glazing.

The control is matched to the window and to ease of use: manual — by crank or switch, or automatic — with an electric motor operated by remote or a wall switch. Automation is especially convenient on hard-to-reach windows and large glazing.

Façade blinds in the Podlasie climate — survey and fitting

In the Podlasie climate, where summer can really beat down and winter bites, this kind of shading works on two fronts. In the heat it protects against overheating, and lowered slats add a layer of air at the glass, which helps cut heat loss on colder days. It's an investment that raises comfort and eases the load on the air conditioning all season long.

Frequently asked questions

How does a façade blind differ from an internal one?
A façade blind is fitted outside, on the elevation, in front of the glass, and stops the sun before it heats the glass — so it effectively protects the interior from overheating in summer. An internal blind controls light and privacy, but the glass has already had time to warm up.
How do you control façade blinds?
Façade blinds are controlled manually — by crank or switch — or automatically, with an electric motor operated by remote or a wall switch. Automatic control is especially convenient on hard-to-reach windows and large glazing.
Do façade blinds reduce room overheating in summer?
Yes. Because façade blinds are fitted outside, in front of the glass, they reflect the sun's rays before they heat the glass and the interior. This greatly reduces room overheating in summer and eases the load on the air conditioning — more effectively than internal shading.
Can you adjust the slat angle on façade blinds?
Yes. The tilting slats of façade blinds give a smooth transition from full daylight to shade. You set them to let in diffused light, reduce it or cover the interior completely, while keeping the view out and privacy from the street side.
Do you fit façade blinds in Białystok on instalments?
Yes. We carry out the fitting with our own Pro-Plast crew in Białystok and the surrounding area, and the purchase can be spread over instalments. The on-site survey and written quote are free and come with no obligation. We match the variant and manual or automatic control to the specific window.
