Insect screens for windows and doors — which one to choose?

The right screen depends on where it has to work and how often you want to open it. You would protect a kitchen window you tilt open ten times a day differently from a patio you carry plates through during a barbecue. That is why, before we quote, we ask a few things: how the window opens, whether there are pets in the house, and whether there is woodland or water nearby — because out by the reservoir or on the edges of Białystok the midges can be far more of a nuisance than in the city centre.

All our window and door insect screens are made to measure, for the exact opening. As standard we use a fibreglass mesh — flexible, UV-resistant and unbreakable in the cold. For cat and dog owners a reinforced mesh (e.g. for pets) is available, which takes claws without tearing.

Below we describe the four types we fit most often: framed, roller, pleated and door screens.

Framed insect screens

This is the most popular and most affordable solution for tilt-and-turn windows. An aluminium frame made to measure from a survey, with taut mesh, clips into special brackets on the outside of the window frame — nothing needs to be drilled into the window itself. We match the frame colour to your joinery: it's available in white, brown or golden oak, so the screen stays discreet.

Removable, so seasonal

Framed screens are removable. You put them up in spring and take them down in autumn in two minutes, then store them in the cellar or garage — no tools needed. This means the mesh does not have to endure winter snow and keeps its clean look for many seasons. It is a good choice for bedrooms, kitchens and living rooms.

  • Aluminium frame made to measure from a survey.
  • 3 frame colours: white, brown, golden oak.
  • Fitted on brackets, with no drilling into the window frame.
  • Matched to any kind of tilt-and-turn window.

If you are replacing your joinery at the same time, it is worth putting it all together — take a look at our PVC windows range and choose your screens on the spot during the survey.

Roller insect screens (cassette)

A roller insect screen has the mesh wound onto a roller hidden inside a small cassette fitted above the window or door. You pull it down when you need it, and at the end of the season there is nothing to take off — the mesh retracts into the cassette on its own and stays tucked away all winter. It is a more convenient and more durable solution than a classic frame, though it costs more.

Who it suits

It comes into its own where you would rather not dismantle and store anything — for example on hard-to-reach upstairs windows, or simply if you do not enjoy the seasonal fuss. The cassette can be colour-matched and the guide rails run smoothly. Versions for balcony doors guide the mesh vertically or horizontally, depending on how the entrance is built.

  • No taking down for winter — the mesh rolls up into the cassette.
  • Smooth, quiet operation and a tidy look.
  • A good compromise for those who want to forget about the whole subject for years.

Pleated insect screens

A pleated insect screen is mesh folded concertina-style (into a pleat), running in a bottom and top rail. You slide it aside like a pleated blind — gently, with one movement — and when it is not needed it folds away to a few centimetres beside the side rail. It looks modern and is very easy to use day to day.

Everyday advantages

  • No threshold, or a very low one — easy to step over, even with a pushchair.
  • Smooth opening with no slamming — ideal for entrances used many times a day.
  • An elegant, slim profile that suits large patio doors and corner glazing.

The pleated screen is a higher-end option — chosen by people who care about comfort and looks. We will advise whether a given entrance is better served by a pleated or a sliding screen, as much depends on the width of the opening and how often you will be passing through it.

Door / sliding insect screens

Patio and balcony doors are a different matter, because we walk through them rather than just tilting them open. A window frame will not do here. We fit two solutions: hinged door insect screens that open like a leaf, and sliding screens that run along a track parallel to the door.

Hinged door screens

They work like a second, lightweight leaf with mesh — you open it, walk through, and a magnet or catch closes it behind you. Handy when you have your hands full. They are well suited to patio entrances in detached houses around Białystok.

Sliding for large glazing

With wide HST sliding doors and lift-and-slide doors the screen also runs along a track — it takes up no space and handles large openings. This is the solution for modern homes with big panes of glass. If you are planning such an opening onto the garden, it is worth thinking about the screen as early as the doors selection stage, so that everything lines up dimensionally.

Measuring and fitting

The most common reason a screen "does not hold back" mosquitoes is a badly taken measurement and a few millimetres of gap down the sides. That is why we do not send customers off with a tape measure — we come and measure ourselves. Measuring at your home is free, as is the quote. On site we check the window type, the brackets, the joinery colour and suggest which mesh will work best in each room.

How working with us looks

  1. You call or write — we arrange a convenient measuring date in Białystok and the surrounding area.
  2. Our fitter takes the measurements and presents a quote — with no obligation.
  3. We make the screens to measure and fit them ready to use.

Framed and pleated screens are fitted without interfering with the window frame, so the warranty on your joinery stays intact. While we are there we can also quote straight away for other shading — roller blinds or venetian blinds — if you want to sort out protection from sun and insects all in one go.

Want a firm price? Book a free survey or call us — we will match the right solution to your windows and your budget.

Frequently asked questions

What colours are framed insect screens available in?
The aluminium frame of the screen is made to measure from a survey and available in three colours: white, brown and golden oak. This lets us match it to your joinery colour, so the screen stays discreet. It suits any kind of window and door.
How much does an insect screen for a window cost in Białystok?
The price depends on the type (framed, roller, pleated, door), the size of the opening and the kind of mesh. Framed screens are the cheapest, while pleated and sliding ones cost more. We give you an exact price after a free survey, with no obligation.
Do you fit insect screens on patio and balcony doors?
Yes. For doors and large glazing we fit hinged door screens, sliding screens that run along a track, and pleated screens that fold concertina-style. With wide HST doors we choose a sliding version or a pleated screen with a low threshold, so you can pass through comfortably, even with a pushchair.
Do insect screens need to be taken down for winter?
That depends on the type. Framed screens are removable — you put them up in spring, take them down without tools in autumn and store them in the cellar. Roller and pleated screens stay in place, because the mesh retracts into the cassette or folds away beside the rail, so it does not have to endure winter snow.
Does fitting an insect screen damage the window or void the warranty?
No. Framed screens clip into brackets on the window frame without any drilling, and pleated screens are fitted without interfering with the sash. As a result, the warranty on your joinery stays fully intact.
